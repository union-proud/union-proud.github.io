Union PROUD for Local 872

Power in Representative Organizing and Union Democracy

We, the Union PROUD (Power in Representative Organizing and Union Democracy) slate, are a diverse group of graduate student workers — international and domestic, humanists and scientists, from UPC and HSC, from departments with 15 workers and departments with 350 workers, and from every current cohort — running for the executive board of our union. Our diversity reflects how our power to improve our working conditions as graduate student workers fundamentally comes from our ability to work together. As we move into this new phase of our local union, we are stepping up as dedicated and ambitious organizers with the experience to hold USC accountable.

Vote Union PROUD May 13 – May 14

Our Vision

Our shared vision of our union is to build long-term, sustainable structures to democratically exercise our collective power as graduate students working for a better workplace environment and as a community standing in solidarity with each other. We know that winning our contract — a historic first for academic workers at USC — is just the beginning: we are ready to build our Local Union from the ground up by creating spaces for graduate student workers to build community and empower leaders, developing clear processes and communication for organizing around grievances, and ensuring all GSWs know their rights and the workings of our union.

Our Commitments

We are committed to building our movement through one-on-one organizing, organizing on grievances, and democratizing our union. We are committed to building collective power and fighting alongside our coworkers to stand up for our rights.

Our Power

Our power as a union comes from all of us. When we join together, we have the power to stand up to USC when they violate our rights. By building a strong Local Union, we also join in the labor movement with over 100,000 academic workers throughout California and around the country. But all of that starts with us. We cannot wait to continue building our union alongside you, and we hope we have your vote.
